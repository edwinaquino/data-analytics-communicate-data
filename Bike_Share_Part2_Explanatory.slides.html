Bike Sharing Trends of Ford GoBike System¶

By Edwin Aquino¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation I wanted to look into how this new form of ride sharing system that has enabled people in the bay area to make use of the service for commuting purposes.

Dataset Overview¶

The data consisted of over 2 million records. The attributes included member's gender, birth year, membership type, trip locations and times. Additional columns were added to deeply visualize data further.

Age Distribution of Users¶

A large percentage of bike users are from the age of 18 to 55. The rest of the users consist of incorrect data as some users show more than 100 years old.

Bike sharing service is highest during the summer months. The weather is more enjoyable to be outdoors, therefore, more people trend to take a bike ride to work to avoid rush hour traffic.

Distribution of Monthly Age Groups¶

This graph shows riders in the 30-40 age group to be the most active. I expected the 20-30 age group to be more active riders. Once possible reason why you see the 30-40 age group is because the service is very popular with commuters. Commuters tend to be individuals with work and more predictable bike riding habits.
